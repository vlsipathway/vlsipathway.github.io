Practical LTSpice: From Basics to CMOS Circuits

Why This Course?

In-depth, Practical Learning with LTSpice

LTSpice is a free yet powerful circuit simulation tool widely used in electronics, VLSI, and IC design. Whether you are a student, researcher, or professional engineer, mastering LTSpice will strengthen your ability to design, analyze, and test electronic circuits.

This course takes you on a step-by-step journey, starting from the fundamentals of LTSpice, building simple circuits, and performing essential analyses. You will then progress toward advanced topics, including **MOSFET characterization**, **CMOS inverter analysis**, and digital circuit implementation such as NAND, NOR, and Full Adder design using **CMOS technology files**.

The focus is practical learning — every concept is explained through examples, hands-on circuit design, and real-world applications. You’ll also gain access to technology files, reference materials, and troubleshooting tips to confidently work on your own projects.

What You'll Achieve:

  • Create, customize, and simulate circuits in LTSpice
  • Perform all essential analyses (.op, .tran, .dc, .ac, .tf, .step, .temp)
  • Work with model files and create custom symbols
  • Characterize MOSFET devices using 180nm CMOS technology
  • Design and simulate CMOS inverters, logic gates, and a full adder
  • Apply LTSpice to practical projects like ring oscillators and op-amp circuits

Who Is This Course For:

  • Electronics and Electrical Engineering students needing strong simulation skills.
  • Researchers and project students working with analog or digital circuits.
  • VLSI and IC design enthusiasts wanting to explore CMOS circuit behavior.
  • Professionals and hobbyists eager to use LTSpice for circuit design and testing.
  • No prior LTSpice experience is needed. Basic circuit knowledge is the only prerequisite.
